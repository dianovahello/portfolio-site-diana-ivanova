Zenvelopes

Zenvelopes is a budgeting app that helps users apply an ancient Japanese budgeting system to their lives, gain a deep understanding of their spending habits, and grow a healthy relationship with money.

Defining the problem

In our historical moment, anyone can monetize their passion and leverage creativity into multiple income streams. The freedom of this can feel amazing, but it comes at the cost of complicating our monthly budget.

We need to manage multiple income streams, large fluctuations from month to month, optional expenses, and fluid circumstances ‐ this is personal finance in the age of makers, nomads, side-hustlers, social media creators, and independents of all walks.

I found that existing solutions were particularly unprepared to help a user who:

  • has unpredictable or negative income
  • has multiple income streams
  • may describe themselves as “not a math person”
  • may experience financial anxiety and procrastination

I explored three existing apps: Rocket Money, Mint, and Ally Bank's Spending Buckets. I found that while they offer powerful features like

  • dynamic data visualization
  • automatic expense tracking,
  • and data‐driven insights,

they fall short because of

  • steep learning curves
  • too many features, and
  • lack of intuitive accessibility.

Moreover, existing apps

  • do not account for multiple income streams
  • do not address financial anxiety, and
  • do not promote personal growth.

I needed a simple budgeting method to address the needs of this problem space: I found Kakeibo.

Kakeibo

Kakeibo is a budgeting system from medieval Japan. Similar to the Western “envelopes” method, it involves recording income and bills, then splitting expenses into separate envelopes (or buckets). But rather than tracking 20-50 envelopes with labels like “Netflix” or “Car insurance”, Kakeibo budgeters divide their expenses to only four categories: Wants, Needs, Culture, and the Unexpected. This minimalist method helps prevent overwhelm, encourages mindful decisionmaking, and increases self-knowledge.

user flow

I stuctured the app along three major flows consistent with a Kakeibo practice. The Plan flow guides the user through inputting their expected income and expenses, planning a savings goal, and setting a time to track and reflect. The Track flow guides the user through the process of inputting real expenses during the month. The Reflect flow helps the user compare their plan with reality in a non-judgmental way and record their insights to the journaling feature. Any flow is accessible at any time from the homepage, and reminders to track and reflect are set by the user themselves.
