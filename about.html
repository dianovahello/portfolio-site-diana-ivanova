About me

download my CV

My path to UX and Product design started with art. Since college I've had a practice in conceptual and media art. It's a great way to use critical thinking, solve unique theoretical problems, and create memorable experiences for people.

I started practicing graphic design in 2016, by creating the print dossier and promotional material for a theater project. I liked applying my skills to solve a practical need, so I began helping more small business owners express their values and connect with their audience. Freelancing helps me stay curious about people, design tools, and business needs, and this made me want to learn more about UX and Product design. I gained skills in UX research and testing processes, rapid prototyping, interaction design, and the basics of web development at CareerFoundry.

What I enjoy most in this field is that I can be curious and creative. It engages all the skills I have learned in my life: visual, technical, and soft.
